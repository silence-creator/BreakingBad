Q01Question

What was Walter White's original salary as a chemistry teacher?

[ tap to reveal ]

$43,700

Walter White earned $43,700/year as a chemistry teacher at J.P. Wynne High School — a salary that drove his desperation.

Q02Question

What color is Heisenberg's signature product, and why?

[ tap to reveal ]

BLUE SKY

The blue color results from the unique synthesis method. It was a creative choice by Vince Gilligan to make it visually iconic and instantly recognizable.

Q03Question

Which episode holds a perfect 10.0 rating on IMDb — the highest ever for any TV episode?

[ tap to reveal ]

OZYMANDIAS

S05E14 "Ozymandias" holds a 10.0/10 on IMDb — the highest-rated episode in television history. Directed by Rian Johnson.

Q04Question

What does "Breaking Bad" mean as a Southern American idiom?

[ tap to reveal ]

TO RAISE HELL

"Breaking bad" is a Southern US expression meaning to go wrong, raise hell, or embark on a destructive path — perfectly capturing Walter's arc.

Q05Question

How many Emmy Awards did Breaking Bad win in total?

[ tap to reveal ]

16

Breaking Bad won 16 Primetime Emmy Awards, including Outstanding Drama Series in its final two seasons (2013 and 2014).

Q06Question

What real chemistry consultant worked on the show to ensure scientific accuracy?

[ tap to reveal ]

Donna Nelson

University of Oklahoma chemistry professor Donna Nelson served as the show's science consultant, reviewing scripts for chemical accuracy.
